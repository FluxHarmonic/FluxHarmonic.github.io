# New Beginnings — Flux Harmonic Relaunch

The relaunch stream. Marking the turn back to creating my own games, tools, and
music in the open, and setting the direction for how I'll build everything from
here: live, in public, with AI in the loop.

## News

- **The new site is live!** fluxharmonic.com got a full rebuild. It's the home
  for everything I'm working on, and over time it'll grow into the public record
  of the work, these stream notes included.
- **System Crafters live streams are going on hiatus.** The live building moves
  here, to Flux Harmonic. If you're here for the live streams, come find me at
  [@FluxHarmonicLive on YouTube](https://youtube.com/@FluxHarmonicLive) and
  [twitch.tv/FluxHarmonicLive](https://twitch.tv/FluxHarmonicLive). System
  Crafters stays put as the home for the Emacs and Guix catalog.

## The Plan

This first one is about the why and the how more than a deep build.

- **What Flux Harmonic is now**, and why I'm bringing it back: a creative label
  for my own games, tools, and music, and a home for building authentically in
  the age of AI. It runs alongside System Crafters, now the legacy Emacs and Guix
  channel.
- **How the pieces fit**: Sigil is the foundation (my own Scheme that compiles to
  native code), the games are the gallery, and a growing set of tools is how it
  all gets made.
- **The recursive part**: the show builds the tools that make the show. Live
  Crafter runs this stream, Easel made the thumbnail, Montage will cut the videos,
  and a multi-agent setup will coordinate the work. You get to watch the machine
  get built on the machine.
- **The roadmap**: a tour of what's coming (see [[topics/roadmap]]), from
  Cinder Cadence and blackICE to reviving the old game-jam games on a new engine,
  pushing Sigil toward 1.0, and getting Enclave and the agent tooling working the
  way I want.
- **How we'll work from here**: out of this folio workspace, in public, with AI
  assistance and (soon) multiple worker storylines running at once.

## Progress Log

- Kicked things off by walking everyone through the plans for the channel: what Flux Harmonic is now, how the pieces fit, and where it's all headed. Rambled a fair bit getting there!
- SummerEmacs is convinced I'm murdering System Crafters. (For the record: it's not dead, it's the legacy Emacs and Guix channel now. The live building just moves here.)
- Started standing up the agent orchestration setup: a focused leader/worker system built on folio (task briefs, knowledge notes) and courier (workers reporting back) so this session can spawn workers on tasks across the Sigil projects. The scaffolding is in place but not wired up for real yet. I want to review it on the next stream before we lean on it, so it's staying uncommitted for now.
